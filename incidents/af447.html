Air France Flight 447

Rio de Janeiro to Paris · June 1, 2009

228 Fatalities
Airbus A330-203
Atlantic Ocean

Flight Overview

Air France Flight 447 was a scheduled international passenger flight from Rio de Janeiro, Brazil to Paris, France. On June 1, 2009, the Airbus A330 serving the flight crashed into the Atlantic Ocean, killing all 216 passengers and 12 crew members.

The aircraft disappeared from radar screens during a storm over the Atlantic Ocean. It took nearly two years to locate the wreckage, which was found at a depth of 3,900 meters (12,800 ft), making it one of the deepest underwater recovery operations in aviation history.

Sequence of Events

The accident sequence began when the aircraft encountered a thunderstorm:

  1. 21:10 UTC: Aircraft enters area of intense turbulence
  2. 21:13 UTC: Pitot tubes ice over, causing loss of airspeed data
  3. 21:14 UTC: Autopilot disconnects, crew loses situational awareness
  4. 21:14 UTC: Aircraft enters aerodynamic stall at 35,000 ft
  5. 21:16 UTC: Aircraft impacts ocean surface at high vertical speed

Key Findings from Investigation

  • Inconsistent airspeed indications due to iced pitot tubes
  • Inappropriate control inputs during manual flying
  • Lack of understanding of aircraft's high-altitude stall behavior
  • Inadequate cross-checking and communication in the cockpit

Safety Improvements

The AF447 accident led to significant changes in aviation safety:

  • Mandatory pitot tube replacement across Airbus fleets
  • Enhanced stall recovery training for pilots
  • Improved cockpit instrumentation and alert systems
  • Development of better data monitoring for oceanic flights
  • New procedures for unreliable airspeed indications

Legacy

The loss of AF447 remains one of the most studied accidents in aviation history. Its investigation revealed critical vulnerabilities in human-machine interaction and led to fundamental changes in:

  • Pilot training for high-altitude stalls
  • Cockpit resource management
  • Aircraft monitoring systems
  • Oceanic flight tracking technology

Memorial services are held annually in both Rio de Janeiro and Paris to honor the victims.
