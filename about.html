About the Author

Dr. James Wilson

Aviation Safety Researcher & Historian

With over 15 years of experience in aviation safety research, Dr. Wilson has dedicated his career to understanding and preventing aviation accidents. His work combines technical analysis with human factors studies to create comprehensive safety recommendations.

Dr. Wilson has participated in numerous NTSB investigations and serves as a consultant for major airlines and regulatory agencies worldwide. His research has contributed to significant improvements in aviation safety protocols.

Professional Qualifications

  • PhD in Aerospace Engineering, Massachusetts Institute of Technology
  • Certified Aviation Safety Officer (CASO)
  • Former Senior Investigator, National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB)
  • Author of "Aviation Safety: Lessons from Tragedy" (2018)
  • Professor Emeritus of Aviation Safety, Stanford University
  • Recipient of the Flight Safety Foundation Award for Excellence (2020)

Research Philosophy

"Every accident is a lesson written in blood. Our duty is to read it carefully and ensure its lessons are never forgotten."

Dr. Wilson's approach to aviation safety focuses on three core principles:

  1. Technical Precision: Rigorous analysis of flight data, wreckage examination, and simulation studies.
  2. Human Factors: Understanding crew decision-making, communication patterns, and psychological factors.
  3. Systemic Improvement: Developing actionable recommendations to prevent recurrence.
